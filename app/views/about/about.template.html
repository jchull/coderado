Why?

I created this site to explore technologies of interest to me. I have developed web applications for the last 8 years, utilizing a diverse set of frameworks, including ADF Faces, Drupal, and Sharepoint. I also worked on the front-end of Oracle ADF, where I learned to love CSS and hate IE-specific javascript bugs.

I think the traditional resume format does not convey the important information quickly enough, it's stale, ripe for replacement. As both a consumer and producer of resumes, I feel the pain at both ends of this out-dated business concept. Personally, I have stuck to the one-page rule, for better or worse I feel like it reduces a lot of the fluff that most resumes have. The crux of this philosophy is that my textual resume may seem small.

Rather than choose one of the obvious options, I thought about the consumers of my resume. It includes a varied list of personas, all with different backgrounds and goals. I left out recruiters, since long term (over 6 months) placement is not easily-incentivized.

  • HR
  • Business Manager
  • Technical Manager
  • Technical Lead
  • Peers
